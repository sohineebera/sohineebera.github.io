Overview

Hello! I’m a 5th year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Communication at Cornell University, where I study how structural and cultural conditions shape participation and voice in science, health, environmental, and risk decision-making. I draw on qualitative methods- interviews, participant observation, and textual analysis- along with surveys to explore this across different contexts.

My dissertation follows female community health workers in rural India as they navigate national health policies, data-driven reporting systems, and the everyday needs of the communities they serve. This work sits at the intersection of critical communication approaches, science and technology studies, and national health policy, examining how structural vulnerabilities and data practices shape care on the ground.

My work has been featured in the Cornell Chronicle and ScienceIRL. I have published in Public Understanding of Science, Ambio: A Journal of Environment and Society, Frontiers in Communication, and the International Journal of Communication.

I’ve also been awarded funding from the Waterhouse Family Institute, Cornell’s Qualitative and Interpretive Research Institue, Cornell’s Graduate School, Cornell’s Department of Communication, Cornell’s South Asia Program, and the National Science Foundation.

I am currently on the academic job market for positions beginning Fall 2027 and am seeking out roles where I can contribute to research, teaching, and community engagement.
